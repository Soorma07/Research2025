# Capital One Behavioral Interview Prep

Capital One places significant emphasis on behavioral interviews, using the STAR method (Situation, Task, Action, Result) to evaluate candidates. They assess alignment with their core values and leadership principles.

## Capital One's Core Values

1. **Excellence** - Deliver best-in-class results
2. **Do the Right Thing** - Act with integrity
3. **Innovation** - Challenge the status quo
4. **Collaboration** - Work together effectively
5. **Customer Focus** - Put customers first

## Common Behavioral Question Categories

### 1. Leadership & Influence
- Tell me about a time you led a project without formal authority
- Describe a situation where you had to convince others to adopt your approach
- How have you mentored or developed other engineers?

### 2. Problem Solving & Innovation
- Tell me about a complex technical problem you solved
- Describe a time you improved a process or system
- How do you approach learning new technologies?

### 3. Collaboration & Communication
- Tell me about a time you had a conflict with a teammate
- Describe working with a difficult stakeholder
- How do you communicate technical concepts to non-technical people?

### 4. Handling Failure & Adversity
- Tell me about a project that failed
- Describe a time you received critical feedback
- How do you handle tight deadlines and pressure?

### 5. Customer Focus
- Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer
- How do you balance technical debt with customer needs?
- Describe a time you had to make a trade-off that affected users

## STAR Method Framework

**Situation**: Set the context (2-3 sentences)
- When and where did this happen?
- What was the business context?

**Task**: Explain your responsibility (1-2 sentences)
- What was your specific role?
- What were you trying to achieve?

**Action**: Detail what YOU did (bulk of your answer)
- What specific steps did you take?
- Why did you choose that approach?
- How did you handle challenges?

**Result**: Share the outcome (2-3 sentences)
- What was the measurable impact?
- What did you learn?
- What would you do differently?
